Symptoms of Fever:

  • High fever (above 38°C or 100.4°F)
  • Chills and shivering
  • Fatigue
  • Body aches
  • Headache
  • Loss of appetite
  • Dehydration
  • Weakness

Recommended Medicines:

  • Paracetamol (Tylenol) - Reduces fever and relieves pain.
  • Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin) - Anti-inflammatory and reduces fever.
  • Aspirin - Helps reduce fever and relieve pain (not recommended for children).
  • Acetaminophen - A pain reliever and fever reducer.
  • Naproxen - Anti-inflammatory for reducing fever and body aches.
  • Diclofenac - Reduces fever and inflammation.
  • Mefenamic Acid - Commonly used for fever and pain relief.

Symptoms of Cold:

  • Runny or stuffy nose
  • Sore throat
  • Cough
  • Sneezing
  • Fatigue
  • Mild fever
  • Body aches

Recommended Medicines:

  • Paracetamol - Reduces fever and aches.
  • Ibuprofen - Reduces pain and inflammation.
  • Antihistamines (e.g., Cetirizine) - Helps with runny nose and sneezing.
  • Decongestants (e.g., Pseudoephedrine) - Relieves nasal congestion.
  • Throat lozenges - Soothes sore throat.

Symptoms of Dengue:

  • High fever
  • Severe headache
  • Pain behind the eyes
  • Severe joint and muscle pain
  • Skin rash
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Bleeding gums or nose

Recommended Medicines:

  • Paracetamol - Reduces fever and pain (Avoid NSAIDs like Ibuprofen).
  • Hydration - Drink plenty of fluids to avoid dehydration.
  • Rest - Essential for recovery.
  • Vitamin C - Helps in recovery and supports immune function.

Symptoms of Malaria:

  • High fever
  • Chills and sweating
  • Headache
  • Muscle pain
  • Nausea and vomiting
  • Fatigue
  • Diarrhea

Recommended Medicines:

  • Chloroquine - First-line treatment for non-resistant malaria strains.
  • Artemether-lumefantrine (Coartem) - Commonly used combination therapy.
  • Quinine - Used for severe malaria cases.
  • Doxycycline - Used for both treatment and prevention of malaria.

Symptoms of Tuberculosis (TB):

  • Persistent cough (lasting 3+ weeks)
  • Chest pain
  • Coughing up blood
  • Weight loss
  • Night sweats
  • Fatigue
  • Fever and chills

Recommended Medicines:

  • Isoniazid - First-line medication for TB.
  • Rifampicin - Used in combination with other TB medications.
  • Ethambutol - An anti-tuberculosis medication.
  • Pyrazinamide - Part of the first-line TB treatment regimen.

Symptoms of Typhoid:

  • High fever
  • Weakness
  • Stomach pain
  • Headache
  • Diarrhea or constipation
  • Loss of appetite
  • Fatigue

Recommended Medicines:

  • Ciprofloxacin - Commonly prescribed antibiotic.
  • Ceftriaxone - Used for resistant strains of Typhoid.
  • Azithromycin - Effective against some Typhoid strains.
  • Oral rehydration solutions - To prevent dehydration.

Symptoms of Stomach Pain (Pet Dard):

  • Cramping or sharp pain in the abdomen
  • Gas and bloating
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Diarrhea or constipation
  • Indigestion

Recommended Medicines:

  • Antacids (e.g., Ranitidine, Omeprazole) - Reduces acidity and indigestion.
  • Simethicone - Relieves gas and bloating.
  • Buscopan - Reduces abdominal cramps.
  • Dicyclomine - Relieves stomach spasms.
  • Loperamide - For diarrhea control.

Symptoms of Heart Attack:

  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Pain radiating to the arms, neck, or jaw
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Cold sweat
  • Dizziness or lightheadedness

Recommended Medicines:

  • Aspirin - Helps reduce blood clotting.
  • Nitroglycerin - Used to relieve chest pain (angina).
  • Clopidogrel - Prevents blood clots.
  • Beta-blockers (e.g., Metoprolol) - Lowers heart rate and blood pressure.
  • ACE inhibitors - Helps relax blood vessels.
